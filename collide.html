Collide is a LA based entertainment platform where fans can interact with their favorite Creators.

DESIGNER - REDESIGN CASE STUDY

What even is Collide?

Collide was a livestreaming platform where influential creators could produce premium content for their audience. Creators could monetized their content through livestreams, video-on-demand, and the ability to exchange calls and texts with fans.

Since joining the team, we have transitioned towards a simpler product model where Creators can upload photos, pre-recorded videos, and call and text with fans.

Identifying Roadblocks Early On

The architecture was derived from an existing website. Our team started to realize there were many technical inefficiency with the product's architecture, which made it difficult to implement certain features that were requested by the investors.

Creators and users were recognized as two different entities. Meaning, Creator accounts could not use the functions of user accounts and vise-versa. This proved to cause major problems in our development process.

Other problems were also quite apparent at the early stages of the initial launch. Retention rate were low, onboarding new fans were slow,revenue stream was lackluster,

Creators were not able to create accounts on their own. They were having difficulties creating and uploading new content.

Fans weren't presented with easily-consumable content.

Big Picture Goals to Meet

My team wanted to lay out the primary goals we wanted to achieve through this redesign. We set up two separate goals, respectively for Creators and fans. We wanted to enhance a fan's experience by improving:

  • Ease of consuming content
  • Finding specific and/or brand new Creators
  • Simplify Login/Sign Up flow
  • A more intuitive and modern feeling product

For Creators, we wanted to improve:

  • Process to create Content as a Creator
  • Easily Sign Up and onboard an account
  • Connecting with fans more effeciently

A New Home

We introduced a Home Feed that showcases featured content so users would have constant content to explore and consume. We also implemented caveats in the home page to encourage users to exlplore other Creators to increase behavior.

As we released the product to the public, we started noticing users would only use the product when a specific Creator they were following were online. The product was not initially built to market content, it was built to market Creators.
